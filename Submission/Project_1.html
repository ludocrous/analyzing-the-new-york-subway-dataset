Section 1.1¶

Which statistical test did you use to analyze the NYC subway data? Did you use a one-tail or a two-tail P value? What is the null hypothesis? What is your p-critical value?

In addition to taking the mean value of 'ENTRIESn_hourly' for both samples, we also perform the non-parametric Mann-Whitney U-test with p-critical of (α = 0.05).

Because either sample could produce larger results we perform a 2-tail Mann-Whitney (α = 0.05)

We use the typical null-hypothesis for Mann-Whitney which stipulates that the probability of drawing a random sample from the first population (which is our sample without rain) being larger than a random sample from the second population (in this case our sample with rain) is 0.5. i.e. the populations are identical.

Hypotheses:

  • H0 : P ( x > y ) = 0.5
  • H1 : P ( x > y ) ≠ 0.5

Where x is a random draw from one sample and y is a random draw from the other sample.

The application of Welch's t-test is indicated where two samples have different sample sizes and potentailly different variances, BUT with the assumption of being drawn from normally distributed populations.

As the p-value returned from is extremely small, we reject the null hypothesis and conclude that neither sample appears to be from a Normally Distibuted population.

This makes Welch's t-test inappropriate, and we perform a non-parametric Mann-Whitney U test (α = 0.05) instead.

Because either sample could produce larger results we perform a 2-tail Mann-Whitney.

Section 1.4¶

What is the significance and interpretation of these results?

With an α = 0.05 and a 2-tail p-value of 0.049999 being in the critical region, we find the difference between the two populations statistically significant and reject the null-hypothesis that they are identical.

Given this, the difference in the mean values of Entries/hr it would suggest that more people ride the subway on rainy days than not.

Section 2 - Linear Regression¶

Section 2.1¶

What approach did you use to compute the coefficients theta and produce prediction for ENTRIESn_hourly in your regression model

We have opted to use the Python implementation of the Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) model from Statsmodels to perform the linear regression.

Section 2.2¶

What features (input variables) did you use in your model? Did you use any dummy variables as part of your features?

Before performing regression we modified the dataset to add two new features:

  • day_of_week: This is set to 0 for a Monday through to 6 for a Sunday and derived from DATEn
  • is_weekend: This is a boolean set to 0 if day_of_week in (0,1,2,3,4) and 1 if in (5,6)

We also created dummy variables for each value of UNIT, which has the disadvantage of introducing numerous variables to the regression model, but dramatically improves the results.

The final selection of features (excluding dummies) was : 'rain', 'Hour', 'fog', 'mintempi', 'is_weekend'

Section 2.3¶

Why did you select these features in your model? We are looking for specific reasons that lead you to believe that the selected features will contribute to the predictive power of your model.

The rationale behind the feature selection was first to focus on those variables we felt could consciously influence a person's decision as to whether to take the subway train or not. These would be conditions that should be immediately apparent to the rider at the time of making the decision of mode of transport, which would exclude those like atmospheric pressure and dew point as the average person is unlikely to know these on any given day.

Section 4 - Conclusion¶

Section 4.1¶

*From your analysis and interpretation of the data, do more people ride the NYC subway when it is raining or when it is not raining?*

Yes, the evidence strongly suggests that on average more people ride the subway when it is raining.

Section 4.2¶

*What analyses lead you to this conclusion? You should use results from both your statistical tests and your linear regression to support your analysis.*

We were able to show that the mean value of ENTRIES_hourly was significantly higher in the sample taken on days with rain, than on those days without. At an α-level = 0.05 our Mann-Whitney U test yielded a probability 0.04999 which allows us discard the premise that difference in the means is solely due to chance.

Analysis of the means in Section 1.3 and Section 3.2 strengthen the argument that more people ride the subway on rainy days.

Section 5 - Reflections¶

Section 5.1¶

*Please discuss potential shortcomings of the methods of your analysis, including: Dataset,Analysis, such as the linear regression model or statistical test.*

The most noteable shortcoming in the dataset, is that it only includes the information for one calendar month and not a full year. Give the impact that weather conditions and holidays may have as we move from season to season, a more accurate model would require a full years data at the very least.

We felt that the R2 coefficient indicating that only 47% of the variability being attributed to the model was not high enough, and that more emphasis should have been placed on feature selection and possibly more categorical classification of data
